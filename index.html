MIT &
Gender Diversity
A representation of gender diversity at MIT through data over the years and what people are saying
Alex Leffell | Rachel Wang | Jessica Wang
4.110 Spring 2014

Women have played a role in MIT for almost its entire existence. The first woman to graduate from MIT was Ellen Swallow Richards, class of 1873, eight years after the opening of MIT.

However, female enrollment rates were almost negligent for many decades, and despite their significant contributions to scientific fields, they were still largely ignored by the general public. Only in recent decades have we observed a jump in womens enrollment.

Today, MIT proudly holds an almost 50-50 ratio of men to women, showing just how far we have come in providing gender equality and opportunities for all.
